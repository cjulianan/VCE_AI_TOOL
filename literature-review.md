# Literature Review
* Source: A Survey on Knowledge-Oriented Retrieval-Augmented Generation (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.10677)
  * Notes: RAG has been used in multiple fields such as pulling information from financial, legal, and industrial texts (Section 8.2). Denoising techniques of dataset such as confidence scoring to reduce hallucinations (Section 5.7). Agentic RAG, most relevant to this project, has limitations such as error propogations (tasks requiring many steps can easily lead to errors), cannot interpret data very deeply (explaining why rather than just give facts), and is not as flexible at retreival planning (may waste resources unecessarily calling API when it can jsut reference data it found previously) (Section 9.4).
* Source: The reliability of replications: a study in computational reproduction (https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rsos/article/12/3/241038/86855) 
  * Notes: Justifies the importance of transparency, citing information from dataset and code to ensure for accuracy rather than guessing. Ways for ensuring for accuracy are (directional reproduction: is the AI's answer in the correct general trend, exact replication: is numerical value within 1% or report, replication error: average absolute difference between AI and dataset answer) (Methods section). Using different programming languages can also have different accuracy (Stata versus R in how they round) (Section 3.1.2). Ambiguous user queries may lead AI to give correct answer for one interpretation but may not be the interpretation user wants (Section 3.1.5).
